Gulab Jamun Recipe

Gulab Jamun is one of the most popular traditional Indian desserts. It consists of soft, deep-fried milk-based dough balls that are soaked in a sweet sugar syrup flavored with cardamom, rose water, or saffron. The dessert is known for its rich taste, melt-in-the-mouth texture, and is commonly served during festivals, weddings, and celebrations across India.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1 cup milk powder
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • 3–4 tablespoons milk (as needed)
  • Now to make sugar syrup use 1½ cups sugar
  • 1½ cups water
  • ½ teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 teaspoon rose water or a few strands of saffron (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the syrup: Boil sugar and water together for 5–7 minutes. Add cardamom and rose water (optional). Keep warm.
  2. Make the dough: Mix milk powder, flour, baking soda, and ghee. Add milk gradually to form a soft dough.
  3. Shape the balls: Roll the dough into small, smooth balls without cracks.
  4. Fry: Deep-fry the balls on low to medium heat until golden brown.
  5. Soak: Place the warm gulab jamuns in the warm sugar syrup and let them soak for 1–2 hours before serving.
